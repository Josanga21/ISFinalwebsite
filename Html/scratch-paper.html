How to do an Ollie

  1. Foot Placement
    • Back Foot: Place the ball of your back foot on the tail, centered horizontally.
    • Front Foot: Place it 1/3 to halfway up the board at a slight angle.
  2. Crouch Down
    • Bend your knees deeply — the lower you crouch, the higher you pop.
    • Keep shoulders aligned and arms slightly back for balance.
  3. Pop the Tail
    • Snap your back foot down to make the tail hit the ground.
    • Jump upward with your whole body at the same time.
  4. Drag Your Front Foot Up the Board
    • Slide your front foot up the grip tape during the jump.
    • Point your toes slightly upward to help level the board.
  5. Tuck Your Knees
    • Pull your knees up, especially the back leg.
    • Keep the board connected to your feet with pressure and grip.
  6. Level Out the Board
    • Flatten your front foot near the nose to level the board.
    • Your back foot will follow as your jump peaks.
  7. Prepare to Land
    • Spot your landing with both feet over the bolts.
    • Bring your legs down in sync with the board.
  8. Land and Roll Away
    • Bend your knees slightly to absorb impact.
    • Stay centered and roll away — congrats, you just ollied!
